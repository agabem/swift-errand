Go-to-Market Strategy

●

Phase 1: Bootstrap & Validate

Months 1-2
  • Launch in single neighborhood (Yaba, Lagos)
  • Manual matching via WhatsApp + mobile web UI
  • Target: 50 signups, 10 completed orders/day
  • Validate unit economics and product-market fit
●

Phase 2: Automate & Scale Location

Months 3-4
  • Automated runner assignment algorithm
  • Expand to 3 neighborhoods (Yaba, VI, Ikoyi)
  • Target: 200 signups, 50 daily orders
  • Implement rating + identity verification
●

Phase 3: Expand & Monetize

Months 5-6
  • Expand across entire Lagos Island
  • Corporate partnerships (restaurants, retail)
  • Target: 500 signups, 200+ daily orders
  • Premium tier + API for merchants

Risk Matrix

⚠️

Runner Safety & Fraud

High Impact / High Likelihood

Runners vulnerable to theft or assault; users may request illegal items

Mitigation: Background checks, insurance, in-app emergency button, trip recording
📍

Unit Economics

High Impact / Medium Likelihood

15% platform fee may not cover infrastructure, support, payment processing

Mitigation: Volume-driven margins, premium tier, B2B partnerships
🔄

Runner Retention

High Impact / Low Likelihood

Runners may leave for better-paying platforms (Uber, Indriver)

Mitigation: Transparent earnings, quick payouts, leaderboards, bonuses
⚡

Regulatory & Compliance

Medium Impact / High Likelihood

FIRS tax compliance, insurance requirements, labor classification unclear

Mitigation: Legal counsel, tax escrow, flexible contractor model
💰

Payment Processing Costs

Medium Impact / Medium Likelihood

Payment gateway fees (2-3%) reduce profitability further

Mitigation: Negotiate bulk rates, explore Flutterwave/Paystack partnerships
📱

Market Competition

Medium Impact / Low Likelihood

Larger players (Uber, Indriver) may copy model

Mitigation: Focus on ultra-local network effects, brand loyalty, niche focus
